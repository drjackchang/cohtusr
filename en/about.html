About the Project

Rooted in Campus, Building Resilience Together: A Sustainable Safety Network

Origin & Core Values

Centered around the concept of "Rooted in Campus, Building Resilience Together," this project applies the 4Rs Framework (Readiness, Risk Reduction, Response, Recovery) to extend social resilience from university courses to community practices.

By integrating the power of professional associations nationwide, we aim to build disaster and emergency resilience among faculty, students, and community residents, ensuring self-help and mutual-aid capabilities during crises.

Project Vision
  • Bridge the gap between campus and societal needs
  • Build disaster awareness in aging communities
  • Revitalize emergency links between hospitals and communities

Strategic Advantage: Group H Priority Power Zone

Located between Taipei Veterans General Hospital and Cheng Hsin General Hospital, our campus holds a unique geographic position. According to Taiwan Power Company's classification, this area belongs to the "Group H: Defense, Transportation, and Essential Users" category.

During rotational blackouts, this zone is prioritized to maintain uninterruptible power supply.

This advantage enables our university to act as a backup center for acute medical care, maintaining robust emergency response capabilities during regional disasters.

Multi-Dimensional Needs Analysis

Campus Needs

Lack of interdisciplinary disaster prevention courses integrated with field practice.

Community Needs

The Beitou/Shipai area has a rapidly aging population requiring stronger disaster awareness.

Medical Needs

Abundant hospital resources exist, but emergency links with communities need activation.

Policy Needs

Government emphasis on community disaster prevention requires active support from universities and NGOs.
